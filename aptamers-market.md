<p><p>The aptamers market can be categorized into DNA-based and RNA-based aptamers. DNA-based aptamers utilize single-stranded DNA to bind specific targets, offering stability and ease of synthesis. They are commonly used in diagnostics and therapeutic applications. RNA-based aptamers, derived from ribonucleic acid, feature unique folding structures that enhance binding specificity and affinity. Their versatility allows for use in various innovative molecular applications, including drug development and biosensing. Both types hold significant potential for advancements in medical and biotechnology fields.</p></p>
