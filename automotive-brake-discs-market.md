<p><ul><li>Drum Type</li><li>Disc Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive brake discs market is primarily categorized into drum type and disc type brakes. Drum brakes utilize a cylindrical drum that houses brake shoes; they are typically found in older vehicles and rear axles of many models due to cost-effectiveness. In contrast, disc brakes feature a flat disc that clamps down on brake pads, providing superior stopping power, better heat dissipation, and improved performance, especially in high-performance vehicles. The choice between the two often depends on vehicle design and intended use.</p></p>
