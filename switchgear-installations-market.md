<p><p>Switchgear installations are categorized into low, medium, and high voltage segments, each serving specific applications. Low voltage switchgear (up to 1 kV) is commonly used in residential and commercial buildings for safety and control. Medium voltage switchgear (1 kV to 36 kV) typically manages power distribution in industrial and utility settings. High voltage switchgear (above 36 kV) is essential for transmission systems, ensuring reliable service in large power grids. Each type plays a critical role in electrical infrastructure.</p></p>
