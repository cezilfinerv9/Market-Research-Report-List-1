<p><p>The multi-mode optical fiber market features different types based on core and cladding diameters, primarily 62.5/125 µm and 50/125 µm. The 62.5/125 µm fiber, with a larger core, enhances light capture but has higher modal dispersion, making it suitable for short-distance communication. In contrast, the 50/125 µm fiber provides better bandwidth and reduced modal dispersion, favoring higher data rates over moderate distances. Both types cater to various applications, including data centers, local area networks, and telecommunications.</p></p>
