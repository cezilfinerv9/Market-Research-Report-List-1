<p><p>Gaucher Disease treatment options primarily include Enzyme Replacement Therapy (ERT) and Substrate Reduction Therapy (SRT). ERT involves administering synthetic versions of the glucocerebrosidase enzyme to compensate for its deficiency, effectively reducing symptoms and improving quality of life. In contrast, SRT works by limiting the production of glucocerebroside, the substrate that accumulates in Gaucher patients, thereby mitigating the disease's effects. Both therapies play crucial roles in managing the condition, offering tailored approaches to patient care based on individual needs.</p></p>
