<p><p>The organic potato starch market is divided into two primary types: native and modified. Native starch is extracted directly from potatoes and is unaltered, retaining its natural properties and functionality, commonly used for thickening and binding. Modified starch, on the other hand, undergoes physical or chemical treatment to enhance its properties, such as improved stability or viscosity. This modification allows for a broader range of applications in food processing, pharmaceuticals, and other industries, catering to specific functional requirements.</p></p>
